About Me

Current position, background, and research focus

I am a learner who finds wonder in how equations can capture the behavior of waves, fluids, and nature itself. Currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Institute of Mathematics, TU-Clausthal, Germany, working under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Philipp Öffner. My research focuses on the mathematical aspects of plasma physics and astrophysical flows, including the design of high-order finite difference schemes for hyperbolic conservation laws, convergence analysis toward dissipative weak solutions, and the development of structure-preserving methods with provable stability guarantees.

I completed my PhD in Mathematics from the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi, under the guidance of Prof. Harish Kumar, with thesis titled "Divergence Constraint Preserving Numerical Schemes for Two-Fluid Plasma Flows."

Research Interests

Entropy Stable Schemes Structure Preserving Schemes Hyperbolic Conservation Laws Two-Fluid Plasma Models Dissipative Weak Solutions Active Flux methods Finite Difference Methods Scientific Computing
